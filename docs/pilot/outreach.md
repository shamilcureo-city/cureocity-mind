# Pilot recruiting — outreach templates

Companion to `docs/PILOT_PLAYBOOK.md` §1. Goal: 5 committed therapists
(solo practitioners or one partner clinic), each with ≥5 active clients.
Recruit 7–8 verbal yeses to end up with 5 who actually do week 0.

Ground rules for every message:

- **Honest about what it is**: an early product, a 4-week pilot, free,
  their feedback shapes it. Never imply an established product.
- **Honest about the AI**: it drafts, the therapist reviews and signs
  everything. Never "AI does your notes".
- **No spam**: warm intros and 1:1 messages only. Every message is
  personal — fill the brackets, don't blast.

## 1. WhatsApp — warm first touch (someone you know)

> Hi [name] — Shamil here. I've spent the last months building something
> for practising therapists and I'm picking 5 people for a 4-week pilot
> before opening it up.
>
> What it does: you run your session normally, it listens (with the
> client's consent), and by the time the client leaves you have a draft
> SOAP note, a clinical brief, and a WhatsApp-ready summary — you review
> and sign everything, it drafts. Works in Manglish/Hinglish, built for
> Indian practice.
>
> The pilot: 4 weeks, free, your real sessions, a 30-min weekly check-in
> with me, and I fix what annoys you the same day. In return I get told
> honestly what's broken.
>
> Worth a 15-minute call this week?

## 2. WhatsApp — referral ask (a yes refers a colleague)

> [Name], one more thing — I have room for [N] more therapists in the
> pilot. Is there one colleague you'd trust to give honest feedback on
> this? A working therapist with an active caseload, comfortable asking
> clients for recording consent. Intro me and I'll take it from there.

## 3. Email — slightly formal version (clinic owner / senior therapist)

Subject: **A 4-week pilot — AI-drafted session notes for your practice**

> Dear [name],
>
> I'm building Cureocity Mind, a clinical co-pilot for practising
> therapists in India, and I'm selecting five therapists for a four-week
> pilot.
>
> The short version: record the session with your client's consent, and
> the system drafts the SOAP note, an ICD-11-anchored clinical brief,
> and a client-friendly summary you can share on WhatsApp. You review,
> edit, and sign everything — nothing reaches the record or the client
> without your explicit confirmation. It handles code-mixed speech
> (Malayalam-English, Hindi-English) as a first-class case.
>
> On data: consent is captured per client, audio is processed in-region
> (India), recordings are purged automatically after processing, and
> client identifiers are encrypted at rest. I'm happy to walk through
> the data-handling in detail on a call.
>
> The pilot is free. What I ask: your real sessions for four weeks, a
> 30-minute weekly check-in, and blunt feedback. I fix reported problems
> the same day where possible.
>
> Would a 15-minute call this week suit you?
>
> Regards,
> Shamil

## 4. Follow-up nudge (one only, 3–4 days later)

> Hi [name] — following up on the pilot. No pressure either way, but I'm
> closing the cohort this [day]. If it's a "not now", that's a perfectly
> good answer and I'll come back after the pilot with something more
> polished.

One nudge. Silence after that is a no.

## 5. Objection cheat-sheet (for the call)

| They say                              | You say                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "My clients won't agree to recording" | Consent is per client, per session — clients who decline are simply seen without it. In practice many say yes when you explain it takes note-writing off your plate so you're more present. Try with 3 clients. |
| "Is this legal / what about data?"    | Built for the DPDP Act: explicit consent capture, India-region audio processing, automatic purge of recordings, encrypted identifiers, full audit trail. Happy to share the data-flow document.                 |
| "AI notes will be generic/wrong"      | The AI only drafts — you edit and sign. If the drafts aren't saving you time by week 2, quit the pilot; that result is useful to me too.                                                                        |
| "I don't have time for a pilot"       | The pitch is that it _gives_ you time — the ask beyond your normal sessions is one 45-min setup and 30 min on Fridays.                                                                                          |
| "What does it cost after?"            | Free for the pilot. The current plan price is ₹999/month — less than one session fee — and pilot therapists keep a founding discount. You'll never be silently auto-charged.                                    |
| "What if it flags something serious?" | Risk cues are surfaced to _you_, in-session or in the note — never to the client, and nothing is auto-escalated. You stay the clinician; it's a second pair of eyes.                                            |

## 6. After a yes

1. Add them to the tracker below and the WhatsApp group.
2. Book the week-0 sitting (45 min, screen-share or in person) —
   `docs/pilot/week0-runbook.md`.
3. Send: the app URL + "have your Google login handy, we'll do
   everything else together on the call".
